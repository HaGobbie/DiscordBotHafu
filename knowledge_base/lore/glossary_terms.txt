--- [Section: アークス] ---
[NGS][PSO2] 1. Name of the organization to which the player character belongs. Also, the individuals who belong to it are called ARKS. Arkus as an individual has a special talent. Not all people who belong to ARCS as an organization are called ARCS as individuals. "Arks" also existed in the old PSO2, and the player character was set to belong to it. "ARKS" is an acronym that stands for "Artificial Relict to Keep Species," and literally translates to "artificial residual species for the preservation of species." Residual species are species that were once widely distributed, but due to changes in the environment, they now survive only in limited areas. In the setting materials and in-game stories of the old PSO2 published in the past, it is said that Arcus as a species was created artificially. For other details, see the Culture/Civilization page. 2. About PSO2 users. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: アークスカード] ---
[NGS][PSO2] A card that allows you to register your own profile in-game. You can edit and check your profile by selecting ``Arcus Card'' from ``Personal'' in the ``main menu.'' On the editing screen, you can edit ARCS cards by combining comments, character appearance, symbol art, stamps, acquired titles, etc. By setting it to public, other players can view it. For details, see the ARKS Card page. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: アークスキャッシュ] ---
[NGS][PSO2] A so-called charging currency that is charged with real money. Often called "AC". For details, see About AC (Arccache). ▲ ▼

--- [Section: アークス拠点] ---
[NGS] An area, such as Central City, that serves as a base for ARKS, one prepared for each region. Lined with various shops and warehouses, you can prepare for your adventure here. Enemies will not appear within the base, and you will not be able to hold weapons. In the past, in the game, when returning from some quests to places like Central City, a message such as ``Press and hold to return to Arx base'' was displayed, but now the specifications have been changed so that it simply says ``Press and hold to return.'' For details, see the Field Equipment/Combat/Status page. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: アークスサーチ] ---
[NGS][PSO2] You can search for other players and their characters by opening the "Main Menu" and selecting "Arks Search" from "Communication". You can search by player ID name, character name, or team name. From the search results, you can also perform whisper chats and friend requests. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: アークスシップ] ---
[PSO2] The ship of the Oracle fleet, which is the setting of the old PSO2. The lobby, shop, my room, etc. of the old PSO2 are all located here. This setting is why each server is called a "Ship". ▲ ▼

--- [Section: アークスヒストリー] ---
[NGS] Quests that can be ordered from the "Quest Counter" installed at each ARCS base. In addition to retrying story quests that you have already cleared, you can also rewatch previously viewed event scenes. For details, please refer to the ARCS History page. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: アークスレコード] ---
[NGS] Content where players compete to see how well they have achieved the target content and aim to obtain better reward items. There is the ``At Random Event Record,'' which is held irregularly, and the ``Team Task Record,'' which is held weekly. For details, see the ARCS Records page. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: アイテム強化] ---
[NGS][PSO2] Weapons and armor can be strengthened in the "Item Lab" to improve their performance. In addition to "N-Meseta" and "N-Grinder", other weapons and armor are required for reinforcement. Depending on the material items used to strengthen weapons and armor, you will receive "enhancement EXP" and the "enhancement value" will increase. For details, see the item enhancement/limit breaking page. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: 青ダメ] ---
[NGS][PSO2][Slang] In NGS, it refers to weak point damage. It is so called because the damage is displayed in blue when attacking a weak point. In the old PSO2, critical damage was displayed in blue, so blue damage meant critical damage. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: 赤箱] ---
[NGS][PSO2][vulgar] Item container [red]. The old PSO2 also has red item containers, but in the old PSO2, boss drop containers and unidentified rare items are called red boxes, and it almost never refers to item containers. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: アクティブスキル] ---
[Game] A skill that exerts its effect depending on the player's operations. Mainly used by setting it in a subpalette. (Can also be set to the main palette) The antonym is passive skill. In NGS, it is used as a class skill classification. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: アクティブソナー] ---
[NGS] A function where Mag notifies you when undiscovered Trainiers, Ryukor Devices, gathering materials, item containers, etc. on the field are nearby. It is also commonly called sonar or mag sonar. See Active Sonar for details. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: アサルトライフル] ---
[NGS][PSO2] Weapon types that can be equipped by Rangers and Gunners. A long-range weapon that allows you to move and attack enemies from a distance. For details, see the Assault Rifle and Assault Rifle/Action/PA pages. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: アシストNPC] ---
[NGS] A feature that targets all combat sections and some emergency quests and trigger quests. Even in rooms with a small number of players, an "assist NPC" will appear and follow the players to provide combat support. Up to 6 people will automatically appear according to the number of players in the room, and the number of NPCs that will follow will be automatically adjusted according to the number of players. Since assist NPCs are not included in the number correction of enemy HP, they are also an excellent force. If you want to fight without relying on assist NPCs, you can disable them from the room keyword settings. For details, see the Field Equipment/Combat/Status page. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: アップドラフター] ---
[NGS] A transportation device for moving to high places. If you Photon Glide inside, you will be swept in the direction of the air current. Basically, all Updrafters installed in the field are facing straight up, but some Trainiers are also facing sideways. See also updrafter. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: アドオンスキル] ---
[NGS] One of the training elements. Create and register add-on skills by consuming "N-Class Excube" and "N-Meseta" at the class counter at each ARCS base, and set the registered add-on skills to achieve the effect. The registration status of add-on skills is shared among characters within the account. Add-on skills are divided by class, and to generate them you will need an "N-Class Excube" with the abbreviation corresponding to the class. (Hunter: N-Hu Ex-Cube, Fighter: N-Fi Ex-Cube...) The generated skills include one fixed "main effect" set for each class and two "sub effects" from random candidates, and the effect size of the skill changes depending on the "effect level" of both. If you repeatedly generate an add-on skill, you will be able to lower the lower limit of the effect level of the generated skill instead of increasing the amount of N-Meseta consumed during generation. Each time you generate an add-on skill for each class for the first time, an "add-on skill boost" will be activated that increases the experience points, N-Meseta, and rare drop multiplier. Once activated, this boost is permanent and stacks for each class. For other details, please refer to the Add-on Skills page. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: アトランダムイベントレコード] ---
[NGS] A type of ARCS record. A limited time event that is held irregularly, where players compete for the score of the target content. Depending on the score, players will be divided into ranks of "Gold", "Silver", and "Bronze", and will receive a reward "Arks Record Badge" according to their rank at the time of the final day's tally. For details, see the ARCS Records page. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: アルターズ] ---
[NGS] An enemy group with a distinctive eyeball-like pattern. It has the ability to change its shape and strength during the day and night, called "Day/Night Shift." See Alters for details. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: 雨乞い] ---
[NGS] [vulgar] Waiting for Giganticus to occur. This is because Gigantics of the Airio region, which was first implemented, uses thunderstorms as a trigger. Waiting for Giganticus in the Ritem region is also called praying for rain, and it is rare to wait for a dust storm. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: 安藤 / 安藤優] ---
[NGS][PSO2][Slang] The main character, the player character. This is a pun on the phrase "And You" written at the end of the credits in Phantasy Star Universe (PSU), which is compared to a person's name. In particular, in PSO2, users can freely decide the name of the main character, so it is sometimes used as a slang term to refer to the main character. There is a sticker that says "AND YOU" in oracle characters for the name tag of the school swimsuit costume distributed in the old PSO2. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: イエローポータル] ---
[NGS] A type of trigger portal. By using this, you can challenge quests classified as "Battle Deer: Yellow". ▲ ▼

--- [Section: 怒り状態] ---
[NGS] A strengthened state that changes when the boss enemy's HP falls below a certain level. Official term used in TIPS when incapacitated. They wear strengthening effects, and if they are dolls, they turn red. There are also enemies whose shape itself changes. As a verb, it can also be used as ``to get angry'' or ``to get angry.'' ▲ ▼

--- [Section: イコライザー] ---
[NGS] A red spiral wedge-like object that pierces Equalizing Enemy's body. Since the Equalizer cannot be destroyed, it is necessary to defeat the enemy itself in order to prevent it from being strengthened by the Equalizing Enemy. See Equalizing Enemy for details. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: イコライジングエネミー] ---
[NGS] A strengthened enemy whose body is pierced by a red spiral wedge-like object called the Equalizer. It has the ability to strengthen the attack power and defense performance of surrounding enemies. See Equalizing Enemy for details. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: 位相空間] ---
[NGS] A space where injured Dark Fals can temporarily escape by teleport. According to Crawford, the Dark False travels through phase space to the docks in the heart of Stia, where it recovers. Note that "topological space" is a technical term originally used in academic fields such as mathematics. In the global version of NGS, the word corresponding to "topological space" is translated as "space-time," which expresses the concept of "space" with a different nuance from "topological space." ▲ ▼

--- [Section: 威力下限補正] ---
[NGS] Weapons have a range of fluctuation in power (more precisely, attack power), and this is an effect that corrects the lower limit of that range. This lower limit can be raised with special abilities such as arms. You can check the blur width in the ``Damage Correction'' column on the second tab of the weapon's item details. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: インスタンスダンジョン] ---
[Game] A dungeon that is generated each time for each party or small group. In NGS, rooms for up to 32 or 8 people are generated each time the exploration and battle sections look like open worlds, and virtually all of them can be said to be instanced dungeons. However, because the exploration and battle sections are created and matched automatically and without the player's knowledge, these are not called instanced dungeons, and cocoons and towers, which are similar to the image of instanced dungeons in MMOs, are sometimes introduced as instanced dungeons. The unfamiliar term "instance" is used in computer science to refer to something that is created and materialized from a template in a way that can actually be used. It seems that dungeon generation is likened to this. ▲ ▼

All Dolls with the name "Vera" wear white armor, have larger bodies than their original species, and have different attack patterns. There is also a special operational preparation ticket that can be obtained as a similar item when a new class is implemented, with item names such as "Slayer Operational Preparation Special Voucher." For specific information on how to obtain and use, please refer to the material item page. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: おつ / 乙] ---
[Game] [General] [Slang] Abbreviation for "Thank you for your hard work." The original word was ``fell down'', which means being unable to fight. It is sometimes written as ``1 Otsu'' to indicate the number of times a person was unable to fight. A synonym in PSO2 is tokopero. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: おま環] ---
[Game] [Slang] Abbreviation for "Your playing environment is bad." It is used to refer to errors, defects, or phenomena that do not occur in other environments or have low reproducibility. It was originally used as an abbreviation to mean "Is your environment the cause of this problem?", such as "It falls off when I do XX" or "It's a maka ring, right?", but it has also become common as in "Is it a maka ring that falls off when I do a ○○"? and "If it doesn't work after trying XX, it might be a maka ring". Please note that if used in a reply, it may be interpreted as an offensive connotation. ▲ ▼

This setting is why each server is called a ``Ship.'' The world of the old PSO2 is sometimes referred to as the "Oracle side" or "Oracle's world." ▲ ▼

--- [Section: ガード] ---
[NGS][PSO2] A general term for weapon actions that allow you to reduce or nullify damage by blocking enemy attacks with weapons. In the old PSO2, it was only set as a weapon action for some weapon types, but in NGS it can now be used with many weapons. An event that can be said to be a type of bullet processing in that it "occurs when it comes into contact with the enemy's attack judgment." Unlike evasion actions, basically, if the attack is not a multi-stage attack, once the attack is blocked, you will not be hit even if you are within the attack range during the duration of the attack detection. However, the evasion judgment for evasion actions remains, and there are also small tricks such as safely connecting from guard to evasion counter. On the other hand, some enemy attacks cannot be blocked by guarding, and are called unblockable attacks. Sword and Katana weapon actions. By holding down the weapon action button, you can reduce damage by consuming a certain amount of PP when attacked by an enemy from the front. The advantage is that if you keep pressing it, you can continue to take a guard motion permanently. Also, by timing the enemy's attack and guarding it, it is possible to completely negate the enemy's attack without consuming PP (Just Guard). If you have learned the skill, a counter will be activated automatically when you use Just Guard. The difference in guard performance is that the sword can be used in all directions by acquiring skills, while the katana can be used in all directions without skills. (When it was first implemented, the katana could only attack from 180 degrees in front, just like a sword without skills, but with the update it became compatible in all directions.) In addition to weapon actions, certain PAs also have timings called guard points. Synonyms: Parry ▲ ▼

--- [Section: ヘイト] ---
[PSO2][Game] Indicates hostile attention from enemy mobs. (The meaning in communication between players is not described.) The action of intentionally raising or lowering hate to create an advantageous situation is called hate management, and it is evaluated as an important player skill, especially in cooperative games like NGS. Hate management basically refers to ``actions that collect hate for oneself,'' and aims to stabilize and improve the efficiency of the battlefield by ``protecting other players by becoming the target of bullets,'' ``increasing attack opportunities for other players with high damage sources,'' and ``gathering multiple enemy mobs in one place and making them targets for area attacks.'' A player whose main role is to collect hate is called a tank. In addition, in order for players other than the tank to support the tank's hate management, it is necessary to take appropriate actions to "redirect hate away from oneself." In the case of NGS, since it is designed so that everyone can play an active role as an attacker, hate management by a single tanker tends to be less stable than in other games. For this reason, it is becoming increasingly important for players other than tanks to have ``skills and special abilities that counter hate.'' ▲ ▼

--- [Section: ヘッドショット] ---
[PSO2][Game] An attack on the enemy's head. It is especially used when attacking with projectiles such as projectile weapons. In many games, the head is set as the part with the highest damage multiplier, and it is not uncommon for a bullet to the head to kill you in one hit, especially in FPS. In NGS, there is no special magnification even if you perform a headshot (unless there is a weak point in the head), so there is no need to be aware of it. ▼ In the old PSO2 In the old PSO2, there was a headshot judgment mainly for shooting weapons, and although it was not as strong as weak points, it was possible to deal a large amount of damage efficiently. However, most enemies do not have head lock detection, and in many cases it is necessary to switch to an over-the-shoulder perspective and aim for the head on your own. On the other hand, impact weapons and legal weapons do not have such a concept, so there is no need to forcefully aim at the head. It often becomes a source of conflict between shooting classes who want to attack efficiently by attaching weak bullets to their heads, and hitting classes who want to attach weak bullets to areas that are easy to hit and aim to efficiently destroy parts or knock them down. There were also balance issues, such as the difference in firepower between when a headshot was taken and when it wasn't, and because there was a headshot, the original attack multiplier had to be set low. For this reason, in high difficulty levels, headshot multipliers were set low, except for boss enemies, and adjustments were sometimes made to increase attack multipliers instead. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: DPS] ---
[Game] [Slang] Abbreviation for Damage Per Second. The original meaning is "the amount of damage that a specific attack can do per second if no preconditions are set." From there, it is also used to mean the efficiency of damage dealt to enemies per hour. It is only a hypothetical value calculated by calculating the damage inflicted according to the catalog specifications, and does not take into account any "prerequisites for an attack to succeed" such as resource consumption, positioning, and ease of attack, so it is difficult to produce the same value in actual battle.If the enemy changes, the enemy's defense power and weaknesses will also change, and the DPS will fluctuate even with the same equipment. In order to compensate for the difference between virtual reality and reality, there is also the concept of DPM, which measures the amount of damage per minute after specifying several conditions. Although it is an index that is somewhat closer to actual measurement because the measured values ​​include shortness of breath due to the amount of PP consumed, it has the disadvantage that it is less objective than DPS because the numerical value is more likely to fluctuate depending on the skill of the measurer and specified conditions, and above all, it is still only a theoretical value like DPS. Related: DPP ▲ ▼

I will not give a detailed explanation of the frame rate as it would be complicated, but if this value deviates from the ``expected value in game design'', whether it is too low or too high, various problems will occur, and if it is extremely low, it will not be possible to operate the game properly.
