--- [Section: 概要] ---
The story (main story) progresses by completing main tasks and story quests that occur from time to time. It also serves as a tutorial, allowing you to learn the operating methods and game system through actual combat. Various functions, shops, moveable regions/sections, etc. are unlocked by progressing through main tasks and story quests. The main task must be completed for each character. If you have a character in your account who has already cleared a specific chapter, you can skip creating a new character until after the specific chapter has been cleared. --- [Section: ストーリークエスト] ---
Quests may occur during the main task. It mainly occurs when important exploration or battle is required in the story. Features You cannot cooperate with other players in story quests, and you must proceed alone. However, the party you formed before the quest started will remain the same. Quests are played in an offline area that is almost the same as the online area, and chats other than party chat will not be received during story quests. In the offline area, the time and weather are fixed, and there are no containers. Ryukor devices will also be quarantined. Among the ARCS bases, only Central City in the prologue becomes an offline map. During this time, weapons can be used in the city. There are restrictions on the areas in which you can move. (You cannot go to another area after starting the quest.) About "Retry" after being defeated in battle By selecting "Retry" from the defeated menu, you will be restarted from the point where you saved midway or from the beginning of the quest. Until the end of the prologue, you will be revived in 0 seconds with the system message "During this quest, you will be automatically revived if you are defeated in battle." (The incapacity menu does not appear in the first place.) If a cutscene is inserted at the start of the quest, you will have to watch the scene again. When retrying, the number of Resta Signs and Reverser Signs held will be carried over, and Resta Signs will be rearranged on the field. Therefore, even if you have 0 Resta Signs and retry, they will be rearranged, so you won't have to worry about getting stuck even if you restart from a mid-save, but you should be aware that it will take time to collect them. Regarding mid-save: A system message indicating that the game has been saved is not displayed, and the existence of the mid-save can only be confirmed by retrying. In most cases, it is thought that a save is inserted into the event scene before the battle with the boss enemy begins. Retries from the first half have been confirmed for both "Dolls of Mt. Magnus" against Nagles and "Bruder Rescue" against Nex Airio. It will be saved when you clear the detailed progress. If you become unable to fight during a quest after saving, select "Retry" from the disabled menu to start from the save point. If you select ``Quest Retire'' from the battle failure menu, you will have to start over from the beginning. If you are disconnected from the server due to logout, line failure, or maintenance, you will have to start over from the beginning. Story quests are long, so try them when you have time. About NPCs who fight together One feature that is common to the NPCs who fight together in some trials is that the HP of NPCs who fight together in story quests basically does not go to 0. Although he may take damage or react to being hit, he will never be incapacitated. There are many scenes where NPCs in classes that use techniques, such as Manon, aim to lower your attributes. It seems that active class skills (such as ``Weak Bullet'' and complex attribute techniques) are generally not used. Similarly, they do not perform special actions that require access to specific objects, such as special weapons or throwing actions. Overall, its combat ability in terms of firepower is not very high, so we cannot expect too much from it. In particular, boss enemy battles tend to be long battles, so by the time it becomes possible to widely strengthen weapons, players will want to approach them with the intention of being in charge of the firepower themselves.
