# Knuckles PA — 基本アクション

## Basic action
A melee weapon specializing in counters that can be equipped by fighters. As you can see, the attack range and attack range are very small, but many of the various attacks involve forward motion, and depending on your skill, you can cover the attack range. The overall movement is second to that of the Twin Dagger, and now that adjustments have been made, there are relatively few gaps, and the maneuverability is appealing. It is designed with a focus on counter performance, such as two consecutive counters using weapon actions and the presence of counter PA. Compared to the other two types of Fi weapons, it has the most unique feel when using it, due to PA's high PP consumption, narrow attack range, and the behavior of normal attacks and PA. In any case, it is a weapon that specializes in counters, so in order to maximize its performance, it is essential to use various counters properly, as well as understand enemy behavior and the ability to judge the situation. Its selling point is its high firepower with counter attacks, and its attack performance against bosses with which it is compatible is quite impressive. However, the attack range and area have very limited means of cover, making it extremely unsuitable not only for group fights against small fish in peacetime, but also for bosses whose weak points often move. Photon Blast is also close to being backward compatible with Twin Dagger and Double Saber, and has low performance in both single firepower and area firepower. Due to these characteristics, its versatility is extremely low when used alone, making it a weapon type that would be somewhat difficult to use on a daily basis. On the other hand, it is very compatible with using Twin Dagger/Double Saber as a multi-weapon and extracting only the counter part of its strength, making it useful from the above two weapon types. Related matters: ``Unarmed (unequipped) combat action'' that was in PSO2 has been abolished. As a result, normal attacks, PA, and weapon actions cannot be used when the weapon palette is empty. As of 23/12, only traces of it remain as an action exclusive to Creative Space.

## Normal attack
A normal attack for “Knuckles”. Raise your fist and perform up to 3 attacks. A normal attack that has a short reach but delivers a quick attack. The 1st and 5th stages are 2 attacks, and the others are 1 input per attack. It can be activated at the fastest speed by pressing and holding the button. Although it is a normal attack, the total power is as high as PA, and even if you use it to collect PP, it will deal enough damage. As you get to the later stages, the pressing distance becomes longer. The first step hardly moves forward, but from the third step forward, it moves forward to a certain extent, and furthermore, if you enter movement input in the direction of attack, the stepping distance increases. Combined with the characteristics of weapon action, if used properly, it can demonstrate aerial combat power comparable to Twin Dagger. The 5th stage of the normal attack has a guard point while jumping up, has an armor effect on the entire motion, and is not interrupted by being hit. Specifically, a guard judgment occurs at the beginning of the motion, persists during the upward multi-stage hit, and becomes an armor effect thereafter. During the final hit while descending, the weapon action (sway) becomes available almost at the same time as the attack judgment occurs, so there is virtually no chance of an opening. If you get hit while in motion, forcefully use it to the end while being armored and hit-stopping. The damage received from the armor part is the same as a direct hit, so be careful if the enemy has high attack power. Overall, it has high performance, but considering that the firing range is short and the motion is long, there are times when it is better to reset the number of stages by inserting the PA so that the 5th stage does not come out. When learning Knuckle Attack Alternation, if you use the 5th normal attack without directional input, it will change to a special attack. The motion is similar to the slamming part of the 5th hit of a normal attack, but as a knuckle attack, it involves a wide range of shock waves. Although the power itself is not high, it is as fast as the first stage, so the DPS itself exceeds the normal fifth stage without this. PP recovery amount is halved as the number of hits decreases. In addition, due to the increased speed, the duration of guard judgment has been significantly shortened. Overall, it has a cycle-oriented performance, and its properties are different from the normal 5th stage. ## weapon action
Weapon action for “Knuckle”. Evade enemy attacks with quick movements. Image Action Sway Power 170 PP Recovery 6 Counter Power 390 PP Recovery 10 Counter Plus Power 500 PP Recovery 10 Knuckle Attack Switch Power 90×3+300 PP Recovery 1×3+3 Attack Switch (Alter) Power 250 PP Recovery 3 Performs a sway with a short invincibility period. After swaying, attack regardless of success or failure. When you input it, you sway on the spot and become invincible, and then you take one action until you attack. If you evade an attack with Sway, the following attack will turn into a special counter move, increasing its power. Furthermore, by learning response skills, when you successfully evade a sway, you can input the weapon action again to generate "Knuckle Sway Counter Plus", and input a normal attack to generate "Knuckle Attack Switch". These two can be used individually even if you have learned both, or you can use them consecutively in the order of Knuckle Sway Counter Plus → Knuckle Attack Switch. Knuckle Sway Counter Plus behaves like stepping in again and performing a weapon action attack. There is a reasonable amount of forward movement, and you are invincible during the motion, and you can also connect it to a weapon action again. If you connect to the knuckle attack switch and get hit, it is also a good idea to dodge with a sway and aim for a counter again. Knuckle Attack Switch performs a technique equivalent to the 5th stage of a normal attack. This 5th stage is more powerful than normal, but there is no invincibility time. It has the same guard point and armor effect as the 5th hit of a normal attack, but the motion is long, and there are many situations where it is more effective to counter again with a weapon action without using this. Regarding the input reception state of the knuckle attack switch The input reception state of the knuckle attack switch does not seem to be released over time. It cannot be canceled unless you change the area by accepting a quest or log out, and it persists until a normal attack is input. After a successful sway evasion, a normal attack is input, and the saved reception state ends when a follow-up Knuckle Attack Switch is issued, or when an airborne normal attack is issued during Photon Glide. Note that only the last successful sway avoidance will be saved. Even if you successfully evade Sway multiple times without using a counter attack, you will not be stocked with more than 2 shots. Also, even if you fail to evade sway while saving a counter attack, you will not lose your stock. After a successful sway avoidance, in addition to movement actions such as jump, step, photon dash, photon glide, and transfer from the world map to each Ryukor device, the input acceptance state will be maintained no matter how many times you use weapon actions (including Knuckle Sway Counter Plus and other multi-weapon types) or use Knuckle PA multiple times. Furthermore, it continues even if you switch to a different multi-weapon type or another weapon type from a different palette, use various attacks or weapon actions with that weapon type, and then return it to the knuckle. Internally, this attack seems to be issued in the form of ``replacing the motion and power while retaining the number of attack stages of the normal attack that occurs during peacetime when input''. Therefore, if you input a normal attack after the knuckle attack switch, the normal attack will be skipped one stage and the next number of attacks will be issued. Example: Sway avoidance → (knuckle sway counter plus) → Knuckle attack switch → Normal attack 2 → Normal attack 3… (The 1st stage of the normal attack that was supposed to come out is replaced and the combo continues) … → PA1 → PA1 → Knuckle attack switch → Normal attack 4 → Normal attack 5 (The 3rd stage of the normal attack that was supposed to come out is replaced by Same Arts and the combo continues) ...→PA1→PA2→Knuckle Attack Switch→Normal Attack 5 (Another Arts replaces the 4th stage of the normal attack that was originally supposed to appear and continues the combo) Knuckle Attack Alternation In the acquired state, the technique that appears will change depending on the presence or absence of movement input, similar to the 5th stage of the normal attack. With either input, the attack switch specifications mentioned above will apply, and the power will be higher than when issued normally. If you end it with the shortened version, the total power will decrease accordingly, but Since the total motion length is also shorter, you can aim to counter attacks at intervals that you wouldn't be able to do in time if you used them normally. If used appropriately, it will lead to increased damage, albeit modestly. Knuckle's mainstay is these counters, which are highly powerful and relatively safe during attacks. I want to actively aim to pick up all of the enemy's attacks. Related class skills Knuckle Sway Counter Plus Knuckle Attack Switch Knuckle Attack Alternation

## photon arts
A fighter's PA is divided into a first half and a second half, and when the first half ends, it can be canceled with another action. If you learn the class skill "Fighter Skip Arts", you can use the second half directly. Combos such as the first half of PA1 → the second half of PA2 are also possible by combining these. Also, although the second half of the PA cannot be canceled, it has an armor effect and is not interrupted by being hit. ## backhand strike
Photon Arts for “Knuckles”. After nullifying the enemy's attack with quick movements, it delivers a deadly backfist to the target. If the nullification is successful, the power increases. Image Lv. 1 Power 255 PP consumption 40 Divided into ``the first half of nullifying (guarding) the enemy's attack'' and ``the second half of performing a backfist''. Unlike other Knuckle PAs, you cannot connect or cancel the first half to other actions, so you will have to use either the "first half + second half" set or the "second half alone". There are no restrictions on how other actions can be connected to a backhand strike. Due to this structure, if you have learned the skill "Fighter Skip Arts", the counter function will not activate unless you use it from the first half. The conditions for acquiring the gauge for the first time in Fighter Arts Pursuit are somewhat special. The skip version is treated as the same as the case where you did not guard in the first half, and the first gauge acquisition is determined separately when the guard is successful and when the guard is successful, rather than just the first half/second half like other PAs. Only in Custom Type 3, if you do not guard in the first half, it is counted separately, and you can obtain the first gauge 3 times when the guard is successful / when the guard is unsuccessful / in the skip version. First half: A defensive action that nullifies the enemy's attack while drawing the left hand. The time required for the nullification judgment is a little over 0.3 seconds, so it's not that difficult to match the enemy's attack. It is treated as a guard and eliminates sustained single attack detection. Guard judgment is effective in all directions, and can protect against attacks from directly behind. If the block is successful, the power of the back fist that follows will be strengthened. This effect will be applied without any problems even if the "target of the guarded attack" and "target of the actual attack in the second half" are different. There is a Just Guard, and at the beginning of the motion (0.1 seconds), and after that, the increase rate in the second half when the guard is successful changes. If the guard is successful, the SE will sound like a guard with a Hu weapon, and it seems to be different when it is just a guard and when it is normally successful. On the other hand, for some reason, cases where the SE is not regenerated even though the guard is successful occur quite often, so it is difficult to tell whether it is successful or not. Second half: Back fist. You can step forward, but the reach is short, and you need to get close enough to the enemy to almost touch it. Furthermore, the raw power is not high. If the guard is successful in the first half, it will be strengthened by about 3 times (3 times when successful, 3.15 times when just guard). Only if the counter is successful, it will move forward a distance of about two steps, and even if it attacks a rushing attack that goes backwards, it can be expected to hit if it is at a certain distance. Guidance is only horizontal, not vertical. The power of a successful counter is extremely powerful for a single attack, and its DPS is 689-781, 883 when just hit, far exceeding the double attack of weapon action counter (DPS 544, 588 when just). When compared to weapon action counters, it is superior in one-shot power and motion speed, making it powerful against high-density attacks, but on the other hand, it is significantly inferior in terms of total power, range, safety, and above all, PP consumption, including follow-up attacks within a single counter opportunity. In particular, the heavy PP consumption is a problem, and if you use it regularly, support with limit break after PP saves, EX skills, subclass skills, etc. is a must. Therefore, it is not simply a substitute for weapon action counters, but it is necessary to understand the differences in their properties and use them properly. If you use the second half directly, there is no guard function, and instead, the attack judgment will occur faster because there is no action to pay off the enemy's attack. The short motion itself can be said to be excellent, and its DPS is also in the high category, but since the PP consumption is enormous, there are limited situations where it can be used effectively. Possible ways to use it include using it as a part for another arts, or inserting it into a short gap. Utilizing the strength of the blow, it can also be used to quickly dispose of small, highly durable objects such as geometric labyrinths by knocking them off the scaffolding. ## photon blast
After turning its body, it delivers a large blow to the space in front of it, and uses the battle between the vacuum wall-like energy force field and the knuckles to deliver multiple hits. In the end, he pierces the force field with his fist, and delivers a massive fist-shaped shockwave that deals huge damage. The motion is difficult to understand what it is doing unless you look closely from the side. To explain it simply, it's like swinging one full power punch over time. Damage processing is as explained above: multiple hits + 1 hit with large damage. The range has been expanded with the update, and Knuckle's attack method now has a wide and long attack detection, but since it hardly moves during the attack and does not track the position or direction, it is easy to miss if activated when the opponent is moving a lot. Think carefully about the timing of activation. Considering power, attack range, accuracy, additional effects, etc., Photon Blast has the most modest performance among Fi weapons. In particular, it is painful that it is now equivalent to backward compatibility with the dagger's Photon Blast, which has similar properties. However, unlike Double Saber and Twin Dagger's PB, it is a fixed point attack that does not move around in a flashy manner towards the sky (in both cases movement towards the sky cannot be controlled or manipulated), so it has the highest ability to aim and hit weak points.
