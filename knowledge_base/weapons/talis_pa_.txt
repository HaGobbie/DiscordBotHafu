# Talis PA — 基本アクション

## Basic action
A legal attack weapon that can be equipped by Force Tector. If you use Photon Arts to deploy Taris around you and then use the technique, you can fire a special bullet that behaves differently than usual. The special bullet fired changes to the same attribute as the technique used. It is also possible to shoot while moving using normal techniques without deploying Talis using Photon Arts, but the movement speed is slower than the rod. A legal attack weapon that can be used by switching between single target technique shooting mode and area technique shooting mode depending on the situation. Deployment techniques will be unified into two types of legal attack shots that behave obediently, regardless of the attributes or type of the original technique. Both of these legal bullets have no concept of distance attenuation or appropriate distance, and are highly effective if you have considerable freedom in firing angle. Due to this property, it can handle a wide range of situations, and its performance superiority is particularly high in group battles in combat sections. Since its actual behavior is that of a "shooting weapon with few gaps," coupled with the evasion performance of weapon actions, it is also a highly safe weapon type during battle. The special technique bullets used by Photon Arts after Talis is deployed generally have low firepower in exchange for performance that emphasizes accuracy, attack range, and ability to continue fighting. It is very good in terms of annihilation power and attribute reduction in group fights against small fish, but as mentioned above, when fighting a single enemy, although it has an attack range that can attack from almost anywhere, high accuracy, and PP efficiency when learning various skills, it has very low firepower. Therefore, it is rarely used for the purpose of power, and it is differentiated from other weapons by its greatest feature, its high attribute reduction efficiency. The drawback is that it has poor counters against enemy attacks, and if you want to perfect its performance in battles against bosses, it will be relatively difficult to use. Evasion performance using weapon actions is excellent, but it is not suitable for use for the purpose of dodging enemy attacks because it does not have counter actions. Step evasion allows you to activate a counter, but the problem is that the step counter's firepower is not particularly high, and the evasion performance of the step itself is low. In the case of Te, it is significantly inferior to Wand in both attack performance and support ability, and it is difficult to use it normally because the shock wave custom of Rapid Smash is sufficient for forward area attack, which is Taris' strength. It is easy to differentiate it from Wand in terms of its sniping ability and down value through coordinate attacks from long distances, so there is no reason why it cannot be used for special operations such as adjusting down value and break gauge. Also, Talis has a very high performance against small fish with Photon Blast, so it is worth considering using it for that purpose. In any case, it is difficult to fight with Talis alone, as it is almost never used for pure firepower. It can be said that it is a weapon that needs to be used properly after preparing a main weapon. It is easy to demonstrate its true value in multiplayer, where it is easy to gain more benefits even if you sacrifice your own firepower. Furthermore, both Fo and Te greatly strengthen their PP-related skills with main-specific skills, so if they are sub-operated, PP efficiency will quickly start to become difficult. If it's for annihilating small fish, which is its specialty, Fo's killing PP gain can significantly strengthen it, but even then, you'll need to replenish PP with Tricky Capacitor or Dash Attack, etc., except during PSE burst. Differences from the old PSO2 Differences from the old PSO2 The ability to place the thrown Talis and fire techniques from there is no longer available. The principle of launching techniques for legal weapon types has been almost unified, and it is no longer possible to use it in any way that is unique to PSO2's Talis. On the other hand, the power of direct attacks by Taris itself, which was almost meaningless, has been increased, and for better or worse, it has become a "normal weapon for ranged attacks." Although there are differences in the presence or absence of PA and weapon action, the difference in the usability of Rod Tallis has narrowed considerably as far as pure techniques are used. ## photon blast
When activated, it is an explosive range coordinate attack that deploys six different-colored tallis in front that appear to represent six attributes, and continues to generate effects and attack judgment in a cylindrical shape at the attack point for a certain period of time. Attack detection is a type that occurs from above the enemy to directly below. It seems that all six attributes are affected by the attack judgment (as revealed by Rappy's attribute destruction effect), and of course attribute down can also be induced. The cumulative attribute down value is extremely high. If it's a boss that has a weak point from the beginning, you may be able to knock it down with your first move. On the contrary, it can be considered that there is no physical down value. Enemies cannot accumulate the same type of down while they are down, so if you shoot while their attributes are down, all the attribute down values ​​will be wasted. If possible, it's best to use it when you want to knock down an enemy, or when it's physically down. All stages have a fairly wide cylindrical area attack, and if the enemy is densely packed, it can also be used for area annihilation. After the upward revision, the attack range was further expanded and this advantage was further refined. As mentioned above, it is a type where the judgment falls from top to bottom. However, the way the judgment occurs is quite straightforward, and even if it is activated from a long distance without considering altitude etc., it will almost always hit the weak point if the target is not the type that moves around. However, it is sometimes difficult to hit the weak point against down motions such as Daidal Sword, which directs the weak point toward the ground, so it often misses the weak point if it is not activated after the opponent's movements have stopped properly. The trick is to not rush and activate it once your opponent has stopped moving. The behavior is a ``coordinate range attack that can be executed from a long distance, ignoring height differences,'' so even if you look at all PBs, the behavior is very straightforward. Even if you target a target with a special weak point, you can easily hit all the weak points by aiming at the moment when it stops moving, so it is extremely easy to use. Previously, it was a PB with outstanding low firepower in exchange for its excellent behavior, but with the adjustment in October 2022, its power has been increased to a medium-low firepower. Having solved the shortcomings, it has become an excellent PB that can be used at any time, regardless of location, whether it is a single boss or a group of small fry. From a force perspective, the power difference has narrowed considerably compared to Rod PB (3840), and the DPS difference has become larger. I want to judge the ease of hitting weak points and the difference in attack range and use them properly. From Techter's point of view, Wondo PB (power 4500) is more advantageous in terms of power, so Wondo is better against a single boss. However, Tallis PB is far more versatile, so there are plenty of ways to use it. On the other hand, Tallis has a significant advantage in terms of attack range, time to deal damage, and attribute down value accumulation efficiency, so there are situations where it is better to use them differently. To be honest, it's a PB that boasts top-class performance, although it can't be said that it's worth the effort of changing hands and creating equipment.
