# Double Saber PA — 基本アクション

## Basic action
A weapon type that can be equipped by fighters and is characterized by high firepower. The reach and range of its basic attacks are somewhat modest, and its raw attack power is also low, but by continuously hitting it with PA attacks, it generates "kamaitachi" around itself, and by adding additional damage from this, it unleashes one of the greatest firepower of all weapons. In addition, it is equipped with a high-speed enemy contact PA, a range attack PA, a PA with a guard point that boasts amazing defensive performance, and each has two types of exclusive attack skills and counters, so even though it specializes in attacks, it also has the dexterity to be highly versatile and able to respond to situations. Of course, in addition to understanding PA characteristics and counter behavior, it requires constant management of Kamaitachi's duration, so it is a weapon that not only requires some getting used to, but also is quite busy to operate. Also, if Kamaitachi disappears due to the passage of time or being unable to fight, its firepower will be significantly reduced, so it is not compatible with situations or quests where it cannot attack for a long time, and there are times when it lags behind other weapons. If you master it, you can reach a very high level of firepower against a single opponent, but as mentioned above, it has a major problem with its key weapon, Kamaitachi, and is relatively difficult to operate. However, compared to the Twin Dagger Knuckle, which requires several steps of operation, it is very easy to operate, and its defensive performance is also very high, making it surprisingly easy to use. A system that generates a circular attack judgment around the player by using Kamaitachi PA and some actions, giving additional damage. By continuing to hit the target action while Kamaitachi is generated, Kamaitachi will grow, and its range and power will increase according to the stage. Only the 1st stage Kamaitachi will occur even if it is a target action. In its initial state, it can grow up to stage 2, and if you have learned Saberwind Extra, it can grow to stage 3. After it occurs, it will disappear in 15 seconds, but you can reset the remaining time by applying a certain number of attacks. The Kamaitachi accumulation value required to raise and maintain the Kamaitachi level is set independently for each type of attack, and the ease of accumulation varies depending on the action. Just maintaining it is possible with a small amount of accumulated value, but moving to the 2nd or 3rd stage requires a considerable amount of accumulated value. When comparing the firepower of fighter weapons, the power of the Double Saber is based on the assumption that it will be maintained at Kamaitachi level 2 or higher. In particular, the third stage is extremely powerful, so the basic idea is to always close the distance and attack continuously to maintain the highest stage, which takes some getting used to. Regarding the effect duration of 15 seconds, most quests have situations where you can attack continuously, so this is not a problem, but in quests where there are gaps or battle sections where enemies tend to scatter, small tricks such as shooting Spiral Drive in the air and resetting the duration will be necessary to maintain firepower. If you switch to another weapon while Kamaitachi is generated, Kamaitachi's damage will no longer occur. This can be fixed by raising or lowering the level or returning to the weapon you were using when the Kamaitachi occurred. For this reason, it is difficult to use tactics such as preparing Plise and other weapon types for each scene and using them properly. Kamaitachi will be maintained even if you use other weapon type actions that have become multi-weapons, but the power of Kamaitachi will decrease during that time. 1/4 of Kamaitachi's damage (more than 1/7 in the 3rd stage only) is added to the damage of attacks from other weapon types, so there is no loss in itself. Kamaitachi's power distribution is 32 per hit in the first stage, 80 per hit in the second stage, and 144 per hit in the third stage. On the multi-weapon side, the first stage is 8 per hit, the second stage is 20 per hit, and the third stage is 20 per hit. ## weapon action
Weapon action for “Double Saber”. Holds both swords and deflects attacks from the surrounding area. Image Action Parry Counter Power 125 x 3 Recovery PP 4 x 3 Another Counter Power 500 Recovery PP 7 Deadly Archer Power 140 x 9 + 168 x 2 (1596) Recovery PP - Action to swing the weapon sideways and nullify (parry) the enemy's attack. If you successfully parry, you can cancel the gap and move on to the next action. The parry reception time is relatively long among Fi weapons, and it is easy to succeed even if the timing is slightly off. When you enter movement input, it will parry while sliding in that direction. It is also possible to change direction and stop while moving. Due to the adjustment, the movement distance has increased considerably to a maximum of 5 meters, and it is now possible to attack the enemy's attack by itself. If you have a target that you can lock onto, you can parry-cancel the first half of Julie Endance and loop it, allowing you to move at high speed without losing altitude. After acquiring the skill, you will be able to activate a great move by holding down the weapon action button. Care must be taken to avoid an explosion. By learning the skill, you will be able to activate two types of counters. The strength of these is that they can counterattack more compactly than step counters. The Saber Parry Counter (normal attack button after parrying) is very fast, has few gaps, has excellent PP recovery ability, and has a high Kamaitachi accumulation value. The motion is shorter than Another Counter, so it is suitable when Kamaitachi is not fully charged or when you want to take counters in succession against small multi-stage attacks. Saber Parry Another Counter (weapon action button after parry) has excellent power and enemy contact ability, and has a long invincibility time. After accumulating Kamaitachi, you will basically use this, but since the motion and recovery are a bit long, in situations where the next attack will come flying at you while you are invincible recovery, it may be better to chop the parry counter into small pieces to gain more firepower. Therefore, it is necessary to use the above two types of counters appropriately depending on the enemy's attack and situation. ## photon arts
A fighter's PA is divided into a first half and a second half, and when the first half ends, it can be canceled with another action. If you learn the class skill "Fighter Skip Arts", you can use the second half directly. Combos such as the first half of PA1 → the second half of PA2 are also possible by combining these. ## photon blast
After setting up, attack while moving forward (3 hits) → Surfing with double saber, spinning attack while rising like a undulation (9 hits) → Cutting off while moving forward (1 hit) → Generating a large tornado (5 hits). Power distribution is 180%*3 continuous attacks + 120%*9 spinning surfing 1560% cutting off at the end + 144%*5 final tornado (18 HIT, total power 3900) It's a wild photon blast with a long motion and a short range, and it moves up and down fairly well, so you should be careful where you launch it. However, after the modification, it became possible to move at high speed and long distances while being activated, making it more effective for cover. Since it is fast and can move almost seamlessly until just before the final stage, it is easy to catch up with enemies that are moving a little. As mentioned above, its movement performance and tracking performance are extremely excellent, and it boasts the fastest and longest movement distance among all PBs, so it has the highest accuracy when it comes to melee weapon PBs. However, as mentioned above, the position increase of the attack judgment during the motion is high (the final landing point is the same as the altitude at the time of activation), so the theory is to activate from a slightly lower position than the target area, especially in the air. Must be familiarized. Of course, the photon blast itself also has the Kamaitachi effect. If you activate Photon Blast in Kamaitachi's third stage, the photon blast will hit 5 times in about 5 seconds, resulting in a significant increase in firepower and DPS, with a total firepower of over 4500 and DPS of 940, which is comparable to a wand. However, currently, Photon Blast alone can go from no Kamaitachi to the third stage, so it has a strong meaning as a firepower boost when there is no time to activate Spiral Drive and Deadly Archer. A Kamaitachi is generated at the same time as the activation, and the Kamaitachi stage increases by 1 stage when all stages of the wild dance are hit, and another stage when the final stage is cut off. If you have time, you can use Spiral Drive → Deadly Archer to consume Kamaitachi, then use Photon Blast to replenish the Kamaitachi, and then use Spiral Drive → Deadly Archer as a combo. For considerations common to Photon Blast, see Weapons/Armor. A common feature of Photon Blast is that it is invincible while in motion.
