--- [Basic action] ---
One of the weapons that Rangers can equip. As the giant weapon looks like, its overall movement is quite heavy, and the openings are also huge, but its great technique, which has difficult openings and movement, has decent firepower even among long-range attack weapons. Although it requires a lot of preparation to activate, Fear Eraser, which uses over 200 PP, is particularly powerful. After the 1/31 adjustment, it has gained the unique strength of being able to reset the Weak Bullet effect time to its maximum value with some attack methods, including this Fear Eraser. Also, it has no special distance, which is common with long-range weapons in NGS, which means that there is no appropriate distance bonus, and the fact that it can continue to deal a certain amount of damage no matter where you attack it is also a big appeal. On the other hand, under normal conditions, it has almost no counter means and, as mentioned above, its movements are slow, so even without the long range advantage, it is extremely unsuitable for tie-down situations with hard medium-sized enemies and bosses. On the other hand, while Launcher Attack Reinforce is active, you will be able to repeatedly fire a powerful step counter that has the highest firepower and rate of fire among ranged weapons. Furthermore, it is still unclear whether it is a cannon or a beam cannon, as it can fire both solid and energy bullets. To be honest, I didn't have reliable range attack ability, but thanks to the custom implementation of multiple launch range attack, I gained a powerful constant range attack ability. If you combine it with Wide Range Weak Bullet, you can get a decent amount of firepower, so it's at a level that you won't have any trouble fighting against a large number of people. A normal attack for the “cannon (launcher)”. Attack by firing explosive bullets. By charging it, it fires multiple explosive bullets to attack a wide area. It is possible to be engulfed by a blast that hits the ground, but unless you are very close, it is difficult to be engulfed. If you hit multiple people with the blast, PP will be recovered by the number of people involved. If you can shoot a non-charged weapon in a densely populated area, you can collect a large amount. Non-charged version: Heavy action that does not look like a normal attack, bullet speed that is not fast, and high power. It is characterized by a long motion that swings the object around with each step. Although the gun muzzle tracks the target until just before firing, the fired warhead only moves straight ahead. The interval between releasing the button and firing is long, and if you're against a small enemy, it tends to miss if you move a little between firing. Although the amount of PP recovery is high, the hit rate is low, so PP recovery often relies on others. Capable of mobile shooting both on the ground and in the air. Weapon action for “cannon (launcher)”. Attach a sticky bomb to an enemy that will explode after a certain amount of time. If you attack the attached enemy, PP will be recovered when it explodes. Image Number of attack hits 0 +n 10 Power 150 +(n*5) 200 Power (wide range) 450 +(n*15) 600 PP recovery amount 50 +(n*5) 100 Shoots a sticky bomb that explodes after a certain period of time after attaching to the enemy. PP recovery is possible. Recast occurs at the same time as firing in 60 seconds (30 seconds when learning quick reload). The attached bomb remains in a standby state for about 20 seconds, and when it deals damage to the target (not limited to the launcher), it is activated and explodes after 10 seconds. Once activated, it will not disappear even after 20 seconds (the explosion countdown will be overwritten when activated) When activated, the blinking interval of the red light emitted by the bomb will become shorter as the explosion approaches. ▼ A short story about targets that can be attached Basically, it is possible to attach, activate, and explode only to targets that cause damage, such as enemies and Tames. If you defeat or destroy the target it is attached to before it explodes, it will disappear along with it. If it is defeated or destroyed just before detonation, it will not disappear and will remain at the coordinates in the air and explode. Due to this condition, it cannot be effective unless it is attached to a ``tough enemy that can survive until it is on the verge of exploding.'' Due to the specifications described below, it is possible to leave a bomb that meets the activation conditions in the air even if the main body is destroyed by ``attaching it to a destructible part and then destroying the target part.'' With the implementation of Sticky Bomb Wide Range, even leftover bombs can be expected to be involved, so it may be effective as an acrobatic. The power of the explosion and the amount of PP recovered increase according to the number of hits made by the launcher that hit the target between the time the bomb was activated and the time it exploded. (It doesn't have to be the bomb's attachment point.) The base power is 150, the base PP recovery amount is 50, and increases by 5 per hit. The upper limit of increase is +50 for 10 hits (power 200, PP recovery amount 100). Effects that increase shooting power and PP recovery amount when attacking are applied. The attack that triggered the activation does not count. However, if a charged normal attack hits 5 times at the same time, 1 hit will be consumed for activation, and the attack will increase by 4 hits (20). The bomb will disappear when you change weapons, but if it is a multi-weapon that includes a launcher, it will not disappear even if you perform an attack/PA/weapon action with another weapon type. PP will not recover unless the bomb explodes and hits. The bomb will disappear if 20 seconds pass without any attack after it attaches, or if you kill an enemy before it explodes. Be careful when pasting in areas where damage may occur. Varas' Iron Fist) However, if the bomb rejoins before exploding, the bomb will return to its attached state. Specifically, after attaching it, you can use it to repeatedly hit main attack PA such as Fear Eraser or Multiple Launch. Image Lv. 1 Power 530 PP consumption 25 Power multiplier is 12.5%*8 A mortar-like PA that drops explosive bullets that hit the target 8 times from directly above the target. --- [Fear Eraser] ---
Photon Arts for "Cannon (Launcher)". Emits a powerful beam of light in front of you. By holding down the PA button, the attack will continue until the PP runs out, and if it continues for a certain period of time, an additional attack will occur. Image Lv. 1 Power 2150 PP consumption 35+1/6F Power multiplier is 10%/hit while irradiating, conditionally activated finish is 40% Effective range is quite long. Penetrates all enemies in the line of fire, and there is no damage attenuation. Because the angle of elevation and depression *2 is extremely narrow at approximately 45 degrees, it does not hit directly above or below itself. During irradiation, the launcher's rotation speed decreases significantly. You can follow fast enemies by irradiating them from a certain distance. Super armor is given during irradiation, but you cannot move at all, so there are many gaps. If you are not confident in your defense power, there will be a corresponding risk. Hit interval is approximately once every 0.5 seconds. If you stop after 7 or more hits, a finish will be activated (a crackling sound will be heard at 7 hits). Due to the long preparation motion, the DPS for less than 7 hits is lower than a normal attack and is the lowest for a launcher, but after 7 hits, including the finish, it becomes the highest DPS for a launcher PA. The PP required for 7 hits is 66 (35+5*6+1) without mitigation. From this point on, the longer the irradiation time, the better the overall DPS of the PA will be, but for this reason, continuously firing at non-weak points is putting the cart before the horse, so in situations where it is difficult to aim at weak points from the front, it is important to use it properly with multiple launch. When finishing, move back a certain distance due to recoil. Targets include Launcher's Weapon Action, Force Skill's Killing PP Gain, and Bouncer Skill's Defeat PP Gain.
