--- [overview] ---
Depending on the enemy's specific attacks, the player character will be burned and damaged, or frozen and temporarily unable to move. This is called an abnormal state. When a status abnormality occurs, the corresponding status abnormality icon will be displayed above the HP/PP bar in the lower left. For the types of status abnormalities, see the list of status abnormalities. After a certain period of time, you will recover from the abnormal status. The icon will flash when the remaining time until recovery is less than 5 seconds. In-game, it is explained under ``Status Abnormalities'' under Main Menu → System → Help → Game Guide → Character Actions/Operations. The player character has a parameter called ``Status Abnormality Resistance'' (described later), which can be checked in ``Basic Information'' under Main Menu → Personal → Character Information. Enemies do not suffer from status abnormalities. Enemies can be put into a special down state (attribute down) with attribute attacks, but this is different from abnormal status. For details, refer to Enemy Down/Part Destruction. Unlike the old PSO2, where status abnormalities occur with a probability, in NGS they occur when a certain value accumulates. ▲ ▼

--- [Attribute down value/physical down value and status ailment resistance] ---
Source: NGS verification data storage Certain enemy attacks have an ``attribute down value'' or ``physical down value'' for each type (attribute), such as burn or freeze, and this value accumulates in the player when attacked. When the accumulated attribute down value reaches 100, it becomes an "attribute status abnormality" of that type, and when the physical down value reaches 100, it becomes a "physical status abnormality". Attribute Status Abnormality Burn Freeze Shock Mirage Panic Poison Physical Status Abnormality Stun Injury ``Attribute Status Abnormality'' is overwritten when another type of attribute status abnormality occurs. “Physical status abnormality” and “attribute status abnormality” occur at the same time. The attribute down value and physical down value accumulated on the player will decrease at a rate of 1 per second 10 seconds after the last accumulated value. Since it decreases continuously instead of once every second (0.1 decrease for 0.1 seconds), it tends to accumulate if you are attacked continuously at short intervals. This reduction speed does not change with the Ranger class skill "Bad Condition Reduce". For attribute status abnormalities other than freeze, if the attribute down value accumulated since the occurrence increases by 100, the level of the status abnormality increases and the time is extended. The status abnormality resistance of armor and special abilities is multiplied by this accumulated attribute down value and physical down value. For example, if you equip 3 pieces of Zeonto Armor (-50% status ailment resistance), 1.5 x 1.5 x 1.5 = 3.375 times the attribute down value and physical down value will accumulate. If the damage taken by a sword or katana guard is increased by 0.25, this accumulated value will also be increased by 0.25. However, even if you learn Braver's class skill "Mighty Guard", which multiplies the damage taken when guarding by 0.1x, it will remain at 0.25x. The reduction in power caused by Valbredder's attack is not treated as a status ailment, and is not affected by status ailment resistance. ▲ ▼

--- [Special environmental resistance] ---
Certain sections have harsh environments, and you will receive unavoidable damage just by being inside the section. The effect can be prevented by imparting "special environmental resistance" that corresponds to the environment. Unlike normal abnormal status resistance, this resistance is additive, and can be nullified by increasing the resistance to +100% or higher. See section page for details. --- [Beaten state (tentative)] ---
A common name that refers to a state where one's posture collapses and becomes unable to take action due to being attacked. Unlike the status abnormalities and special downs mentioned above, there are some that occur for both players and enemies. (The system may be in a different state.) ▲ ▼

--- [knockback] ---
A short-lived hurt motion that occurs when receiving an attack that has a knockback effect. Other terms include flinching, leaning back, staggering, and staggering. In the old PSO2, it was described as a knockback. As a player, you will basically flinch if you are attacked. It can be nullified by launch/blow-off prevention effects such as Massive Hunter (Super Armor, etc.). Small enemies will flinch if you hit them with an attack, but enemies larger than a certain size, such as Pedas Sword, will flinch only if you hit their weak point. Boss enemies are completely disabled. ▲ ▼

--- [Launch/Blowout] ---
A state in which you are hit in the air by being hit by an attack that has a launch/blow-off effect. Official terminology used in the description of Massive Hunter. If you fall to the ground in this state, you will be knocked down, but by inputting a jump while being hit, you can adjust your posture in the air and avoid being knocked down. (Passive) The difference between launch and blow-off is only the initial speed and is not distinguished. The motion is also common to the rotation of the aperture. There is also an attack that knocks you down when you are hit by a bullet, but this has the same effect as a blowback, and the downward blow knocks you down immediately. In the case of enemies, only small enemies such as Tinos are effective, and enemies larger than a certain size such as Pedas Sword are disabled. ▲ ▼

--- [down (fall)] ---
He was blown away and fell to the ground. The hit detection (hit detection) has become considerably smaller, and enemy attacks that are about to hit may not hit you, but you are not invincible, so don't let your guard down. Also, while down, it becomes super armored. By inputting a jump when the character is down (the grace period is long), you can jump up and avoid being knocked down. (Passive) In the case of enemies, this only occurs on enemies that do not have the above-mentioned blow-off resistance. Unlike the player, launch/blowdown is effective even when down. In addition, if Galagarongo hits an obstacle during a spinning attack, it will self-destruct and go down on its own. Officially, it is used interchangeably with the various types of down mentioned above, such as in the description of Scout Wasp, where it is expressed as a down state, but it should be noted that it is a completely different state. ▲ ▼

--- [Hold state (tentative)] ---
A condition that occurs when receiving attacks called grab and hold techniques used by some enemies. In the end, he is thrown or slammed, but the motion of being thrown is also a series of hurt states, making it impossible to receive a passive attack in the air. After being knocked down, the hold state is released, allowing for passive attacks on the ground. Can be disabled with super armor attached to PA etc. However, it cannot be prevented by the effects (sturdiness) added by skills such as Massive Hunter. Basically, you can escape by repeatedly hitting the direction input (lever gacha). It will also be canceled if the enemy interrupts its attack motion due to a special knockdown. The main users are the Cragbear type and Chiaculio type. Ikusubjin's grab attacks are not hold attacks, but seem to be stiffened by hit stops, which will be described later. Currently, the player does not have an attack method with a hold effect, so this will not occur to the enemy. ▲ ▼

--- [hit stop] ---
A phenomenon in which movement stops for a moment when receiving attacks that do not have the hit effect mentioned above, or when blocking the hit effect with super armor, etc. *1 The motion is not obstructed during action, and the motion continues even after the stop ends. Ikusabjin's grab attack, Ikusaengoku's restraint attack, Rasetsu's special sword restraint, etc. seem to have extremely long hit stops. Unlike the hold state, the player remains rigid without changing his posture, and even if the enemy interrupts the attack due to being knocked down, the rigidity period will continue until a certain amount of time has passed or another attack is received. Also, if the player is restrained just before the invincibility time expires due to a player's action, the character may remain invincible and remain rigid until the restraint is released. ▲ ▼ Displaying the latest 20 items.
