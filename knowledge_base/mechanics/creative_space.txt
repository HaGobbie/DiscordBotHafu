--- [Section: 概要] ---
Island as a stage Architecture example: House Implemented on June 7, 2023 (Team space implemented on August 2 of the same year) Creative space is so-called housing-based content. Main Task After clearing the ``first mission'', the function will be unlocked by completing the main task related to ``Request from ARCS Architectural Researcher'', which will be guided by ARCS Architectural Researcher Arche. Arche will also explain the basic operations and functions through side tasks, so let's proceed with this first. On your own vast island, ``Creative Space,'' you can use ``Build Parts'' to create your favorite structures. You can create not only rooms and houses, but also monuments, athletics, and gigantic structures, and even manipulate the undulations of the terrain. If you find it difficult to create it on your own, you can also use the "blueprint" function to build it. You can own one space per ship and account (+1 for premium sets), and you can purchase rental rights for additional spaces with AC. In addition, ``Team Space'' has been implemented in Team, which has almost the same functionality, and there are also exclusive parts such as team mugs. Creative Space One free space per account Premium C Space A space that can only be used during the "premium validity period" Rental C Space 30-day ticket (700 AC), 90-day ticket (1200 AC) Team Space One free space for each team with 4 or more members You can enter Premium C Space and Rental C Space even if the usage period has expired. ``Build parts'' come in a variety of forms, including various building materials, furniture, objects such as trees, and equipment with special functions such as teleporters.In editing, ``build parts'' can be rotated (so-called X, Y, Z axes), scaled (enlarged/reduced in size), and colored (for some component parts). Build parts can be used by consuming registration items from the C space menu and registering to your account. Some build parts can only be traded in My Shop when they are in the "registered item" state. See Build Parts for a list of build parts. Major build parts can be purchased using Genesis Points (GP). Some parts and collaboration-related parts are available at AC shops and AC scratches, and there are also a small number of parts that can be obtained through login bonuses, special scratches, etc. ``PSO2'' room goods cannot be used, but some have been renewed and distributed as ``Build Parts'' for ``NGS'' specifications. Most of these are sold at Star Gems, and both GP and SG parts can be obtained from the purchase menu in the space. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: 島の基本仕様] ---
Common specifications: The rock wall on the outer periphery and the terrain outside it cannot be changed. The height of the ground is not uniform compared to the inner circumference, and the shape does not follow the grid. Build parts can also be placed on the outer periphery of the island. If parts are placed floating on the ground around the outside, it is necessary to deal with this by setting "Snap to grid" to OFF. Emergency quest notifications do not depend on the theme, and are from the Airio region. Since the world map cannot be opened inside the space, emergency quests that occur in other regions must be checked outside. If you get stuck due to getting caught in a build part, etc., you will be transferred to the initial teleporter after a certain period of time. If you enter the terrain on the inner periphery, you will be redirected to the specified position on the rock wall on the outer periphery. Related world setting information On the planet Halfa, the sun rises in the west and sets in the east with a southern orbit. At 5 o'clock and 19 o'clock, both day and night lighting effects disappear and the area becomes darkest. The sun and moon appear above the horizon at around 6:30 and 19:08. Airio Theme The inner periphery cannot be dug down to the sea level. Water comes out when you dig down to the lowest elevation, but the elevation at this point is about the same as the rock ledges around the island. (Added on 2023/6/19) *Since the vertical and horizontal XY axes have not been confirmed, they are written as [Axis 1, Axis 2] Reitem Theme Compared to the Airio Theme, the difference in height from the outside is smaller, and the depth that can be dug into the inner periphery is also very shallow. Water will still come out if you dig to the lowest possible elevation, but it is also the same that you cannot dig to the same height as the desert outside. Kvaris Theme An island shaped like an island with a gentle spiral slope on its outer periphery. Compared to Airio and Litem Theme, the default island area is slightly smaller, and the outer periphery does not completely surround the island, allowing you to walk along the inner periphery and go directly to the sea. Its unique feature is that the inner circumference can be dug all the way to sea level (the water surface that appears at the digging limit is the same height as the sea surface). Far Eastern Theme A map that stands out from previous themes, consisting of six large and small floating islands floating in a sea of ​​clouds. If you fall from the floating island, you will be returned to the floating rock at the top, the return point. Except for the small rock at the return point, it has a pot-shaped shape surrounded by bedrock, and if you dig deeper into the ground surface, you will find a water hole at the lower limit. Although the surface area is not very large, the challenge is how to utilize the floating islands that are arranged three-dimensionally. Furthermore, the terrain between the floating islands is an area where climbing and descending is prohibited, so it is not possible to connect the ground to make one island. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: 設計図端末：設計図] ---
By accessing the build part "Blueprint Terminal", you can open the UI related to blueprints. Blueprint terminals cannot be installed in "Team Space". To register a blueprint, you need to install a blueprint terminal from editing. The process involves registering a group of installed parts together in a blueprint in a specified space near the terminal. There is a link of "one blueprint per device". Specifying the space is done by modifying the coordinates. You can experience the entire process in Arche's side task ``The Path to Master Creator IV.'' Please note that you cannot expand the range behind or below the blueprint terminal. Range specification fails if the range includes items that cannot be included in blueprints such as message packs. There is no flexibility to register items other than specific items within the range, so it is necessary to register them as blueprints before installation. If the range includes items that use blueprints, designation of the blueprint range fails. Please note that you cannot save a new design as a new design after remodeling using a blueprint. If you are thinking about distributing it, you need to keep in mind the layout including the blueprint terminal when assembling it. If you have the space, it would be a good idea to create a room specifically for distributing blueprints, separate from a room for everyday use that emphasizes usability and scenery. If you download a blueprint that uses parts that you have not registered, the unregistered parts will be displayed as a list and you will be asked whether or not to purchase them. (For parts that cannot be purchased with GP/SG, such as AC items or task reward items, only that fact is displayed and there is no guidance to the purchase screen.) Saved blueprints are managed for each ship. Please note that if you plan to move the ship, the original product will be completely rebuilt from scratch. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: コネクトシステム] ---
"Connect System" is a function that links different build parts together. If you select "Send/Receive" in "Connect Send/Receive" in "Build Parts Settings", you can make detailed settings for linking with other parts. This allows you to create field race-style gimmicks such as doors that open and close with switches, lights that turn on and off, and symbols that continuously acquire and appear. Connect ID can be specified as you like in the range of "001" to "999". The assigned ID will not be listed, so we recommend that you take a separate note of it. Even if you send to a Connect ID that does not have a role, or send to a useless action such as "close" a "closed door", no error message will be displayed. There is a slight delay in sending and receiving connections. If multiple connects send/receive at the same time, an excessive number of connect send/receive messages will be displayed. If an excessive connect transmission/reception message is displayed, all transmission/reception of the gimmick parts currently being connected will stop, so be careful if you are installing consecutive gimmicks. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: 設計図] ---
"Blueprint" is a function that supports the player's construction. By saving the "blueprint" registered on the blueprint terminal, you can install a building according to the "blueprint" as a type of build part. If you want to save and publish your own building, you need to install a blueprint terminal and specify the area, and other players can use the same by saving the "blueprint" from that terminal. In addition, from Menu: Creative Space, you can use the ranking function of the most commonly used "blueprints" and the search function that allows you to find "blueprints" based on several conditions. Anything copied using a "blueprint" is treated as a single unit, regardless of the number of parts it consists of. It is not possible to partially modify the component parts. You can add parts later. The blueprint that can be saved in the official C space is the building in front of the space without the decorations, which is a good example of a functional experience. When deleting parts, if you accidentally target something in the "blueprint", the entire thing will be deleted, so we recommend careful operation. Build parts must be available to reproduce the "blueprint". If it includes build parts that can be purchased with GP/SG, you can also purchase the necessary parts in bulk when copying. Even if there are missing parts, you can copy the "blueprint". It can also be installed without missing parts. Parts installed from the "blueprint" can only be rotated on the Y axis. Also, the scale cannot be manipulated at all. This is not a big problem for buildings, but you need to be careful when mass-producing decorations based on blueprints. There is no cost required to download the ``blueprint'', and the ``blueprint'' provider does not receive any compensation. Please note that only the basic settings of the build parts included in the blueprint will be saved, and all connect settings will no longer exist. It is also impossible to change the settings after creating a blueprint. We recommend removing build parts using Connect before saving the blueprint. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: 高度差による設置可能位置の細かさの変化] ---
As the altitude increases beyond a certain level, the possible installation positions on the Y axis become rougher. Near the highest altitude, there is such a difference that even the Y-axis movement using the fine adjustment function becomes visibly coarser. For example, in the Air theme, there is a difference in altitude between just above the sea level and the default initial teleporter, and then there is also a difference in altitude at the level of a double dash jump. Therefore, if you want to assemble detailed blueprints, you can create items with even more detailed vertical positions by creating them near the sea level. Installation using blueprints can ignore this roughness, so if you really want to build something with a detailed design at a high altitude, it might be a good idea to install it using a blueprint at a low altitude. Furthermore, the projected installation silhouette in the blueprints is affected by the roughness of the altitude difference, so depending on how it is made, the silhouette may look amazing. ▲ ▼
