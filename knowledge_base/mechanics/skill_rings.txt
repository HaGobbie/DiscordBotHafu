--- [Section: 概要] ---
Implemented on October 8, 2025 Equipment that is classified as armor that exerts its effect when the "described conditions" are met, such as EX special abilities. There are ``Type 1'' that mainly reinforces firepower, and ``Type 2'' that reinforces defense and efficiency, and one of each type can be equipped from the ``Armor'' window. Rings can only be attached, removed, and changed within the ARCS base. The name is "Type ◯/(Skill Ring Name)". Unless otherwise noted, it can be obtained through limited-time quests and limited-time emergency quests after the implementation date. Unlike the recent drop policy, it seems that once implemented, it will not be dropped in all content distributed in the future. A few types are picked up for each quest, and others do not drop during that quest. ▼ Addition/Change History 2026/04/08 Added the following skill rings Type 1/Count Rise Type 2/Count Follow 2026/04/01 Added the following skill rings Type 1/Photon Blast Rise Type 1/Defeat Rise Type 2/Photon Blast Follow 2025/10/22 Update Added the following skill rings Type 1/Under PP Rise Type 1/Patient Rise Type 2/Under PP Follow Type 2/Patient Follow 2025/10/08 Update Added the following skill rings Type 1/Swish Rise Type 1/HP Key Prize Type 1/Killing Rise Type 2/Swish Follow Type 2/Over HP Follow Type 2/Simple Guard ▲ ▼

--- [Section: タイプ1／HPキープライズ] ---
The more HP remaining, the more power increases, up to +3.00 (4.00/5.00/6.00/6.50/ 7.00 )% / Recovers 1.00 (2.00/3.00/4.00/4.50/ 5.00 )% of HP every 20 seconds. The power increase effect is applied as long as HP is 100%. When the remaining HP is 90% or more and less than 100%, the effect will decrease to 90%. After that, the effect will decrease by 10% (0.3/0.4/0.5/0.6/0.65/0.7%) each time the remaining HP decreases by 10%, such as less than 90%, less than 80%, etc., and the power increase effect will be disabled while the remaining HP is less than 10%. Due to this structure, it is more appropriate to say ``the less HP remaining, the lower the power'' than to say ``the more HP remaining, the more power increases.'' Although the activation conditions are strict, there is no specification of the effect target. HP recovery effect does not activate when HP is 100%. Although it says ``Recover every 20 seconds,'' the count does not start until HP decreases to less than 100%, and in reality, the effect is ``When HP decreases, it will recover by the indicated percentage every 20 seconds until it is fully recovered.'' Therefore, it is not compatible with EX turnover alone. It goes well with configurations that make it easy to keep HP 100%. On the other hand, configurations that reduce HP (see Type 1/Patient Rise) are not recommended. Te, which has recovery methods other than Resta sign, Br, which can recover passively, Wa, which recovers when hit and can save on Resta sign consumption, and long-range weapons in multiplayer where it is difficult to get hit. The effectiveness of EX Try Shield (+ EX Avoid Onslaught composition with high affinity with it) Type 2/Patient Follow depends on the maximum HP and the degree of damage taken, but it is effective if it can take down one shot. ▲ ▼

--- [Section: タイプ2／カウントフォロー] ---
When an enemy is hit 30 times, recovers HP by 1.00 (2.00/3.00/4.00/4.50/ 5.00 )%, recovers PP by 4 (8/12/16/18/ 20 )%, recast for 10 seconds. The nature of the recovery effect and recast is Type 2/Swish Follow. It is similar to , but the advantage of this is that it is activated just by attacking, regardless of the opponent's actions. The HP recovery effect is activated even when HP is fully recovered. There is no time limit for hitting 30 attacks, and the count will not be reset under any conditions other than when the effect is activated. For example, even if you wait for 1 minute after attacking 20 times, the recovery effect will be activated after the next 10 attacks. The number of hits made during the recast will not be counted at all, and you will need to make another 30 hits after 10 seconds have passed. Therefore, the actual activation interval is the recast time plus the time it takes to hit 30 times. When combined with EX Turnover, which has an effect duration of 20 seconds, the effect ends if you do not hit 30 times within 10 seconds after recasting. It is relatively easy to use for Gunners and Fighters who require a lot of moves, but it is difficult to constantly activate it with just the ring effect for weapon types that do not earn many moves. ▲ ▼

The following is preliminary information *1 *2 provided by the development team in a media interview. As an image, it will be something like "Skill Ring" from "PSO2". As the "third equipment" after weapons and armor, it will be possible to equip two slots at the time of distribution. In "PSO2", it was positioned as an external class skill that functions in a specific class, but in "NGS" it is planned to have an effect similar to an EX special ability. Even if the effect is the same, there are variations that can be activated in a way that suits your play style, such as ``activate when evading,'' ``activate when PP is consumed,'' and ``activate when HP is maintained at a certain level.'' Skill rings in PSO2 were made by collecting gathering materials, but in NGS they are dropped directly as rings. The probability is that it will require a certain amount of play, but since it can be used immediately after obtaining it, it is easy to test its effects. If you end up wearing the same ring, you can use it to strengthen the ring so it doesn't go to waste.
