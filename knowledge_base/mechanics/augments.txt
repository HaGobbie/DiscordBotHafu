Adding special abilities requires special ability capsules and N-Meseta. Special ability capsules can be obtained from enemy drops, special ability capsule exchange, treasure shops, etc. The types of special ability capsules that drop differ depending on the section and enemy type. See Details for more information. Preset abilities, which are given to dropped weapons and armor with a low probability, are different from special abilities. Dasha Diable I~III Added Dru's Sole III 2021/07/07 Added Thunder Weaker I ▲ ▼ Additional success rate Special ability capsules have an additional success rate set for each type. You can increase your success rate by consuming up to 10 capsules of the same type at the same time. For example, the additional success rate of Power I is 10%, so if you use 10 of them, it becomes 100%. You can also increase your success rate with auxiliary items. For additional success rates for each type of special ability, see the ``Success Rate'' column in the Special Abilities List. S Capsule Some special ability capsules have an "S" at the end. The rarity has been changed from the original capsule, and its special ability is treated as a different type from the regular product without S. This is mainly relevant when referring to the number of types of special abilities, such as the latent ability ``Skill Type'' or the preset ability ``Fixer Abundance''. This capsule has a 100% success rate and can always grant special abilities with just one consumption. It can be obtained from Treasure Shop SG, limited time SG Shop, specific AC scratch bonuses, etc. ▲ ▼ Number of special abilities that can be added (number of slots) The number of special abilities that can be added (number of slots) is determined by the reinforcement value of the equipment. For details on strengthening equipment, see Item Enhancement/Limit Breaking. For example, if a total of 4 parts (weapon and 3 parts of armor) have Power III (2.0%) attached, it will be 1.02 x 1.02 x 1.02 x 1.02 = approximately 1.082, and the power will increase by approximately 8.2%. See also damage calculation. Special environmental resistance effects are added. For example, if 3 parts of armor have Decord Standard III (25.0%), 0.25 + 0.25 + 0.25 = 0.75, and environmental damage will be reduced by 75%. ▲ ▼ Inheritance of special abilities Once added, special abilities will not be lost unless they are overwritten. The inheritance success rate is always 100%. Therefore, it is not necessary to succeed in all additions at once, and it is possible to add multiple times. Since the equipment itself cannot be used as a material, special abilities cannot be transferred to other equipment. ▲ ▼ Special ability transplant Implemented on June 5, 2024 Related > Announcement "Special ability transplant & N-Special ability transplant pass (Players site) Features available in the item lab. By consuming N-Special ability transplant pass x1 and 10,000 N-Meseta, it is possible to transplant special abilities (all or EX special abilities only). N-Special ability transplant pass is item recycling You can exchange it for 100 N-Special Abilities Transplant Pass Supplementary Tickets that can be obtained from title rewards, etc. ① Transplanting all Special Abilities If the number of special ability slots in the base item and material item is the same, or if the base item has more, it can be transferred regardless of the series or weapon type. All special abilities of the base item will disappear and be replaced with the special abilities of the material item. If the number of special abilities of the material item is less than the slot of the base item, all remaining slots of the base item will be empty. All special abilities of the material item will disappear and become empty slots. ② Transplanting EX special abilities Only if the number of EX special abilities of the base item and material item are the same, the normal special abilities will remain for both the base item and the material item. All EX special abilities of the base item will disappear and be replaced by the EX special abilities of the material item.If you want to attach an EX special ability to a weapon that does not have an EX special ability, you will need to port it first.Due to the specifications of ①, if the weapon you want to attach an EX special ability to has any ability, it is strongly recommended that you port it first. With the implementation of EX special ability A in the form of a capsule on January 1, 2026, it has become possible to retrofit EX frames with a complicated procedure and additional costs.For details, see the specifications for EX special ability A.Weapons can only be transferred between weapons and armor.Currently equipped items cannot be selected and transferred when selecting a material item, and all the special abilities that have been granted. There are weapon series that can be transplanted without consuming the N-Special Abilities Transplant Pass.Transplanting using these as material items (transfer source) does not consume the pass (Meseta is consumed).It supports both "transplanting all special abilities" and "transplanting EX special abilities". The base item (transfer destination) can be anything. On the other hand, even if you select these weapons as a base, if the materials are not compatible, the porting pass will be consumed as usual, so be careful. Weapon series that do not require a pass for porting are listed below. ★11 ``Eredimu'' series ★12 ``Claudim'' series ★13 ``Hubert'' series ★13 ``Vorheur'' series ★13 ``Eindale'' series ★14 ``Spradio'' series Currently, compatible weapons have one thing in common: ``EX special abilities are initially given at the time of drop.'' ▲ ▼ Auxiliary items These auxiliary items are consumed regardless of the success or failure of adding special abilities. The (apparent) success rate increases if you use them all at once, but by using one capsule at a time, you can add ``N-ability additional success rate + α%'' each time. In conclusion, if you use success rate increasing items and insert capsules one by one, the expected value per capsule will increase, but the probability of not being successful at all increases. In other words, if you repeat inserting one capsule 10 times, you will succeed two or more times = the possibility of making multiple equipment with that ability will increase (If you try 18% success rate 10 times by combining one capsule with an 8% success rate and a 10% success rate, the expected value is that you will complete 1.8 pieces of equipment with that ability, but there is a ``probability of not succeeding at all'' of about 13.74%). 0.82 x 0.82 x 0.82 x 0.82 x 0.82 x 0.82 x 0.82 x 0.82 x 0.82 x 0.82≒0.1374 Conversely, if you put in 10 items at once, the probability of success increases, but no matter how you try, you will only complete one piece of equipment with that ability. (If you use 10 capsules with an 8% success rate and a 10% success rate to achieve a 90% success rate once, the expected value is to complete 0.9 pieces of equipment with that ability, but the probability of failure is suppressed to 10%.)
