A shooting class that uses Gun Slash (PA) as its only exclusive weapon. Stylish and dynamic battles unfold, including rapid continuous attacks that interweave various actions. Due to the characteristics of Gun Slash PA and class skills, although it is a shooting class, in reality it mainly focuses on close combat. Overall, it has excellent mobility, and while it has long invincibility evasion actions and class skills, its basic HP is the lowest among all classes. (Reference: Status/Class Comparison) Although it has excellent firepower, mobility, and evasion, it can be said to be an attack-specialized class that is vulnerable when hit. Since its basic HP is low, it is very effective to follow up its HP with armor, special abilities, and add-on skills. If you cover it properly, durability issues can be suppressed to the point where you don't have to worry about it. If you prepare properly, you will enjoy good operability with few quirks. On the other hand, the exclusive weapon Gun Slash has a gear gauge, which is rare in NGS, so it requires the ability to manage that gauge (at the same time as managing the active skill's gauge and CT).Furthermore, the PA is divided into stay version and move version, and there are a wide variety of counter actions and types, so there is a lot of need to memorize and think about choices during battle. The difficulty level of operation is quite high because the slash pass that is activated by the normal attack input when using PA is required almost every time, so the operation is very busy. Based on the above characteristics, the overall review is that this class is suitable for intermediate level students and above. Many of the class skills are related to increasing the critical rate, and when used as both main and sub skills, firepower can be stably increased without activation conditions. Based on these facts, it can be inferred that the name "Slayer" referring to this class was one of the ideas proposed when Raster was in its development stage. In the March 29th broadcast of "NGS Headline+" on the global version's official YouTube channel, the global version's community manager said that "Slayer" was chosen from several name candidates given by the Japanese development team.
