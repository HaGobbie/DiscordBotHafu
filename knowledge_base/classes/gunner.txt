--- [Section: 概要] ---
The official abbreviation is Gu (Gu nner). It can be equipped with two types of weapons: an assault rifle (PA) that is good at medium-range attacks, and a twin machine gun (PA) that has high evasion and is good at short- to medium-range attacks. Due to his special skills, he will basically be using twin machine guns as his main weapon. However, the main class correction will also be applied to assault rifle related skills that can only be used as a sub-ranger. (Rifle Grenadier, etc.) A class with excellent mobility and evasive power that makes full use of twin machine guns, and can deal high damage at short to medium range. It is good at short range where the Twin Machine Gun's damage bonus is greatest due to its distance aptitude, but it can do sufficient damage up to medium range. In addition, it is able to use area attack PA with excellent tech arts customization and homing dart of assault rifle, so it is strong against multiple battles and can respond to a wide range of situations such as close, medium, and range. The most distinctive feature is the class skill Chain Trigger, which increases the number of chains by attacking during activation, and by finally applying Charge PA, it is possible to generate a chain finish that exhibits extremely high damage and down value. By using this chain finish, it is possible to exert damage and down value that exceeds that of Photon Blast, so the key to firepower is how to effectively generate chain finish in conjunction with the powerful active skill Hail of Bullets. In terms of counters, its characteristics are different from other classes, and it has special counter behavior tied to chain triggers, such as earning the number of chains with the Stylish Roll Counter Bullet that is activated when an attack is successfully nullified by a weapon action, and aiming for even higher chain finishing damage by activating the Stylish Roll Strike Back, which is stocked by the successful nullification of an attack by a weapon action, at the time of a chain finish. In a multiplayer environment, the selling point is the DPS role, which can consistently demonstrate extremely high firepower due to the increased power due to the long range advantage and the aforementioned high response and attack abilities. However, due to the slow initial response due to the chain boost specification and the inability to apply the long range advantage, the performance is modest in boss tieman type content that ends in a short time such as various duels and arc records. Also, if you can continue attacking at your own pace, you will be able to exert great power, but if you lose your pace by missing the chain finisher, which is the key to your firepower, your attacks tend to become sloppy. Although it has overall high performance, it has many characteristics that are different from other classes, and it can be said that it is a class for intermediate players and above, as it requires some getting used to. --- [Section: チェイントリガー] ---
Activated by hitting an enemy with charged PA, and for a certain period of time, the number of chains increases each time you hit an attack. In this state, if you hit the charged PA again, a chain finish will be activated that increases the damage dealt for a certain period of time depending on the number of chains. *Main class/twin machine gun only Image Lv. 1 - - Chain counting and chain finishing only apply to attacks with twin machine guns. Attacks with other weapon types, including weapons added with multi-weapons, are completely excluded. A chain count starts by hitting the charged PA of the twin machine guns, and from then on, the count increases by the number of hits with various attacks from the twin machine guns. The counting time is 30 seconds, and when the remaining time is running low, the count display will blink. The upper limit of the count is 100. The count is based on the number of hits regardless of the enemy or part, and attacks that hit containers, ore, trial transport trucks, etc. are also counted. If you hit Charge PA again while the count is displayed, Chain Finish will be activated. When Chain Finish is activated, the count display will change to yellow, and a damage bonus will be given to attacks at the time of finishing and attacks during the period when the count is yellow. Power multiplier is base multiplier 150% + 1% added for each chain. At count 100, it becomes 250% (2.5x damage). When acquiring "Chain Trigger Increase", the count is 150 and it is 350%. A 15 second recast occurs when Chain Finish is activated. Recasting can be shortened by learning the "Chain Trigger Quick Reload" described below. If the reception time passes without finishing, the recast will not occur, and you can immediately start the chain by hitting Charge PA again. The power increase during chain finishing does not apply to Photon Blast and Hail of Bullets. Also, most attacks using gimmicks on the field are not targeted. On the other hand, there are some gimmicks such as Sky Charger and Plasma Trap that have an exceptional chain finish correction, and if utilized, they become extremely powerful attack methods. Due to the adjustment in 2025/8, the effect of chain finish now also affects the down value. A key skill for twin machine guns. There are many derivative skills, and with the exception of some attacks, the firepower has been adjusted to be based on this skill, so you can't start without using this skill first. For specific combo examples during chain finishing, see here. Considering the balance with various skills, the conventional wisdom was that the firepower efficiency would be improved if Chain Finish was activated at around 100 counts, but after the adjustment on August 6, 2025, Chain Finish at around 150 can now produce higher DPS. For this reason, unless you are already in a situation where you have around 150 chains, you can say that Hail of Bullets can be used immediately as soon as the gauge is filled and there is no problem. This is because Hail of Bullets has a slightly long cooldown, so even if you save it, you won't be able to activate it in time for Chain Trigger's CT to end. It's basically better to finish after activating Stylish Roll Strike Back, but you need to be careful if it seems like it will take more than a few seconds from reaching count 150 to take Strike Back. Make early decisions. Considering the balance with Hail of Bullets, which is another key to firepower, and Hail of Bullets Amplify, which has a certain effect regardless of the count, when dealing with an opponent who leaves the battle area for a long time of ten seconds or more, such as in the second half of Dark Falz Souras, it is effective to perform a chain finish without missing even if the count is moderate. Recasting takes at least 2 seconds from the end of the finish. (When learning "Chain Trigger Quick Reload". If you take into account the charging time of the chain starting PA, it's actually less than 1.5 seconds) It's pretty easy to judge what to do during this recast. Since Cloth Bullet has been greatly strengthened, it is basically a stable choice to have a charged version of Cloth Bullet in between. For higher firepower options, sub-rangers might consider a rifle grenade or spread shot. ▼ Adjustment/Modification History 2025/08/06 Changed so that the chain finish down value increases according to the number of chains. 2021/12/15 Fixed a bug where attacks other than twin machine guns could start or end chains, or increase the number of chains. 2021/10/13 The power of Chain Finish has been revised upward. Gauge cannot be accumulated for photon blast, dash attack, and dive attack.
