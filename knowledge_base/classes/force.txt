--- [Section: 概要] ---
The official abbreviation is Fo (Force). It can be equipped with a rod (AC) that mainly uses techniques to attack from a distance, and a tallis (PA) that changes the performance of techniques. In addition, both weapons can be used to activate complex techniques, which are great firepower skills. Along with Techter (Te) and Bouncer (Bo), this class can actively aim to lower attributes. Since the technique is a pure attribute attack, it is easier to accumulate attribute reduction values ​​than the other two classes. If the technique is an enemy's weak point attribute, the power increases by 20%, and you can only aim to lower the enemy's attribute by attacking with the weak point attribute, so it is especially important to use techniques that correspond to the enemy's weak point attribute. It has one of the longest range attacks, and can deliver a certain amount of firepower even from long distances. Since it is a long-range weapon, its firepower under normal conditions is average, but it has a skill that greatly increases the power of techniques against downed enemies, and has high rush firepower when downed. Its normal firepower is on par with other classes, and on top of that, it is equipped with two types of active skills and high DPS gauge techniques, making it extremely strong in battles that end in a short amount of time. In addition, it is possible to steadily and continuously distribute damage + attribute attacks of a certain amount to a group of enemies, and the range attack performance of the combined technique is extremely excellent, making it extremely strong against group battles. It has many class skills that assist in PP recovery, and its DPS is less uneven. If you set it as a subclass, other classes can also benefit from it. It has the lowest HP and lowest defense after Slayer (Sl) among all classes, so be careful not to get hit. While Rod is good at long-range attacks, the DPS of counter actions (Elemental Bullet) and step counters during weapon action guard are quite low. Long battles cannot be avoided, and in order to provide high firepower against raid bosses where counters are the mainstay, it is necessary to make full use of counters using guard points of some actions, and short-range techniques depending on the attribute. Unlike other weapon types, the rod's technique activation motion, parry, and counter action do not have the property of forcibly moving, so the player can move around while adjusting the position at will. Coupled with the Elemental Bullet's homing performance and the range of its techniques, it is able to fight by ``positioning itself to easily understand the enemy's motion and counterattack'' rather than ``taking a position to hit a weak point with a counter attack,'' making it excellent at short-to-medium range combat in ways other than firepower, which is different from melee classes. Careful maneuvering is required, but the high-performance rod guard and the presence of Rod React Advance mean that when equipped with a rod, it is difficult to die even if you move roughly. It is up to the player to decide whether to maintain stability with a low firepower but excellent weapon action guard, or aim for a severe but powerful counter using some techniques and rod attack impact guard points. On the other hand, Tallis has almost no counter means, only step counters, but it has the characteristic that when a technique is used after PA, it changes to an attribute bullet, allowing it to fire stable attacks and wide-range attacks with the same properties regardless of the attribute. Although its firepower is low against a single target, its strength is its stability, allowing it to continue firing almost guaranteed attacks over and over from a safe distance and high altitude. You can reliably accumulate damage even against enemies that move violently. Depending on the selection of Tech Arts Customization, the rate at which the attribute reduction value accumulates will also be at a fairly high level. Regardless of whether you use Rod or Tallis, unless you are trying to push the limits of performance, you are basically a class that only uses sniping techniques from long distances, so it is very easy to operate. Therefore, this class is highly recommended for beginners and those who are not good at high-speed close combat. ▼ Adjustment/Modification History 2021/10/13 Update HP has been revised upward Force subclass Force EX style Force as a subclass Comments

--- [Section: グランツグリッター] ---
By applying the light attribute technique to an enemy multiple times, a ring of light will develop around itself for a certain period of time. During deployment, if you use a technique with the same attribute, an additional attack will occur depending on the technique. If you deploy the ring of light again while it is being deployed, the effect will not overlap, and instead, the effect time of the ring of light that is currently being deployed will be reset. Image Lv. 1 - - Common skill of Force, Tector, and Bouncer. The halo lasts for 10 seconds. Additional attacks vary depending on the technique, so refer to Technique/Light Attribute. If it is a light attribute technique, it can be deployed and maintained regardless of whether it is charged or uncharged, and additional attacks do not consume dedicated resources, so after deploying the light ring, you can fight without worrying about its operation. The deployment technique by Talis PA cannot deploy and maintain the ring of light, and no additional attacks will occur. Pursuit with the Jet Boots Element Set allows you to deploy and maintain a light ring. The number of hits required for deployment and maintenance is not simply the number of hits, but a cumulative value set for each technique's attack judgment is counted each time it hits, and when it reaches a certain level, it will be deployed and maintained, which seems to be similar to physical down/attribute down. For example, Granz can deploy the ring of light in 4 hits with either charged (4 attacks) or non-charged (1 attack), but if he continues to hit the charged version only for the first hit, he can finally deploy it on the 19th hit. On the other hand, if Gi Grants (charged and non-charged attack twice) hits many enemies at once, it can be deployed in one attack. The hit count during the development of the ring of light is maintained even after it disappears. For example, if you hit the non-charged version of Grunts three times during deployment and then disappear, you can deploy it again just by hitting the non-charged version of Grunts one more time.
